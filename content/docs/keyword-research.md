## Understanding Keyword Metrics

### Search Volume

Number of monthly searches for a keyword:

- **0-100**: Very low (niche topics)
- **100-1,000**: Low (specific queries)
- **1,000-10,000**: Medium (good opportunities)
- **10,000-100,000**: High (competitive)
- **100,000+**: Very high (extremely competitive)

**Strategy:**

- Target mix of high and medium volume keywords
- Don't ignore low-volume if highly relevant

### Keyword Difficulty

How hard it is to rank (0-100 score):

- **0-30**: Easy - New sites can rank
- **31-50**: Medium - Requires quality content
- **51-70**: Hard - Need authority and links
- **71-100**: Very Hard - Dominated by major sites

**Strategy:**

- New sites: Focus on 0-30 difficulty
- Established sites: Mix of 30-50 difficulty
- Authority sites: Can target 50+ difficulty

### Cost Per Click (CPC)

Average price advertisers pay per click:

**Interpretation:**

- **High CPC ($3+)**: Commercial value, high intent
- **Medium CPC ($1-3)**: Moderate commercial value
- **Low CPC (<$1)**: Informational, less commercial

**Use Case:**

- Affiliate/commercial content: Target high CPC
- Informational content: CPC less important

### Search Intent

What users want when they search:

**Types:**

**1. Informational**

- User wants to learn
- Example: "how to meditate"
- Content: Guides, tutorials, explanations

**2. Commercial Investigation**

- User comparing options
- Example: "best meditation apps"
- Content: Reviews, comparisons, lists

**3. Transactional**

- User ready to buy/act
- Example: "buy meditation course"
- Content: Product pages, sign-up pages

**4. Navigational**

- User looking for specific site
- Example: "headspace login"
- Content: Brand/product pages

**Strategy:** Match your content type to search intent

## Keyword Research Strategies

### Long-Tail Keywords

Longer, more specific phrases:

**Benefits:**

- Lower competition
- Higher conversion rates
- Clearer user intent
- Easier to rank

**Examples:**

- âŒ `meditation` (too broad)
- âœ… `guided meditation for anxiety beginners` (specific)

### Question Keywords

Keywords phrased as questions:

**Common Patterns:**

- How to...
- What is...
- Why does...
- When should...
- Where can...

**Benefits:**

- High informational intent
- Featured snippet opportunities
- Natural for voice search

**Example:**

```
"How to start meditation as a beginner"
- Volume: 1,200
- Difficulty: 28
- Intent: Informational
- Opportunity: Featured snippet
```

### LSI Keywords

Latently Semantic Indexing - related terms:

If main keyword is `meditation`:

- LSI keywords: mindfulness, breathing exercises, relaxation techniques

**Benefits:**

- Improve content relevance
- Avoid keyword stuffing
- Rank for multiple terms

### Competitor Keywords

Find what your competitors rank for:

1. Enter competitor URL
2. View their top keywords
3. Identify opportunities:
   - Keywords they rank for that you don't
   - Keywords with weak content
   - Gap opportunities

## Keyword Research Workflows

### For New Content

1. **Research Phase**
